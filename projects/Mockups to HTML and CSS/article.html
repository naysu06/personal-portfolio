Tubbataha Reef

The Tubbataha Reefs is a marine park located in a remote area in the Sulu Sea, off the coast of Palawan Island. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993, the park is considered by many divers as the best diving site in the Philippines, boasting extraordinary marine biodiversity. The park features an untouched coral reef with a perpendicular wall that reaches 100 meters, vast lagoons, and a couple of coral islands. The Amos Rock Site is a diver's paradise, where you can admire corals, schools of giant trevallies and groupers, various shark species, and a wall covered in gorgonians.

The best diving season at Tubbataha Reefs is from March to June when the sea is calm, and visibility ranges from 30 to 45 meters. The water temperature ranges from 26°C to 30°C. This is recommended for those with an Advanced Diver and Nitrox certification, and experience in diving with currents on walls.
